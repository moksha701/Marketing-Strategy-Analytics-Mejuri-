# Stack the Deck — Integrated Marketing Campaign for Mejuri

**Created By:** Moksha Shah  
**Campaign Type:** Conceptual Integrated Marketing Campaign  
**Target Audience:** Gen Z & Millennial Women (20–35)

---

## Purpose of the Project

Developed a cross-platform marketing campaign concept for Mejuri focused on positioning jewelry as everyday self-expression rather than occasion wear.

The project combines:
- Social media strategy
- Influencer marketing
- Email marketing
- Paid advertising
- Performance analysis

Main objectives:
* Increase engagement through platform-native content
* Drive traffic and conversions through Reels and creator content
* Demonstrate how creativity and analytics work together in modern marketing campaigns

---

## Campaign Concept — “Stack the Deck”

A game-inspired campaign using chessboards, playing cards, and editorial styling to create a playful yet elevated luxury aesthetic.

> Every piece is a power move. Every stack is a strategy.

---

## Channel Strategy

| Channel | Strategy |
|------|----------|
| **Instagram** | Editorial Reels, flat lays, BTS content |
| **TikTok** | Creator-led styling videos and trend content |
| **Influencer Marketing** | Micro + mid-tier creator partnerships |
| **Email Marketing** | Launch + abandoned cart campaigns |
| **Paid Media** | Meta Reels ads + Google Search ads |

---

## Key Insights

| Insight | Finding |
|---------|----------|
| Reels Performance | 35% spend generated 48% of traffic |
| Influencer Strategy | Micro-creators drove highest engagement |
| Email Marketing | Subject Line A outperformed by +10pts |
| Attribution | Influencer traffic impacted by UTM gaps |

---

## Key Recommendations

1. Shift more budget toward Instagram Reels
2. Expand micro-influencer partnerships
3. Improve campaign tracking & attribution
4. Increase UGC-focused content
